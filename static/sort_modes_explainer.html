Bins whose first four positions share the same item pattern are placed next to each other. This surfaces "archetypes" — clusters of bins with a similar opening profile — as broad horizontal colour bands. Positions 5 and beyond do not affect the grouping.

Use when: you want to discover which bins naturally belong to the same category, without knowing the categories in advance.

Bin Rank

Bins are ordered by their global rank — the best-ranked bin (rank 1) sits at the top. The item content of each row is unchanged; only the vertical order reflects performance.

Use when: you want to see whether high-performing bins share a distinct item profile compared to lower-ranked ones.

Mode 4

Top-rank

Bins are sorted strictly left-to-right: first by what item is at position 1, then — among bins tied at position 1 — by position 2, then position 3, and so on. The result is that the leftmost column is perfectly grouped; later columns resolve ties progressively.

Use when: you want to find bins that have an identical item sequence from the very first position.

Mode 5

Selected Share

After highlighting one or more items using the item pills, bins are ranked by how many of the visible positions those items occupy. The bin where your chosen items appear most often rises to the top. When two bins tie on count, the one where the selected items sit at earlier, more prominent positions ranks higher. Changing the position range filter updates the ranking immediately.

Use when: you've selected a specific item (or a set) and want to quickly find the bins most dominated by it — and, among equals, where it appears most prominently.

Example: Red is selected. Bins are ranked by how many of their 5 positions are Red; ties go to the bin with Red at earlier positions.
